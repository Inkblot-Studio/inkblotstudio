# Production Brief and Go/No-Go Packet

## Purpose

Convert concept planning into a production-ready execution framework with clear ownership, dependencies, and launch gates.

## Scope for Production Kickoff

- Premium interactive web experience focused on enterprise positioning and qualified lead conversion.
- Key modules: hero, capability narrative, case proof, CTA conversion flow, trust anchors.
- Technology direction: progressive 3D enhancement with performance-safe fallback architecture.

## Workstreams and Owners (Template)

- **Strategy and Messaging:** final narrative, proof hierarchy, CTA copy.
- **Creative Direction:** visual language, motion principles, experience coherence.
- **Experience Design:** interaction flows, information architecture, fallback UX parity.
- **Engineering:** rendering architecture, performance implementation, accessibility compliance.
- **Content and Proof:** case artifacts, outcome metrics, testimonials, credibility assets.

## Phase Gates

### Gate 1: Strategy Sign-Off

Required:
- KPI framework approved.
- Persona model approved.
- Narrative spine and CTA system approved.

Exit criteria:
- No unresolved positioning conflicts.
- All stakeholders agree on conversion objective hierarchy.

### Gate 2: Experience Concept Sign-Off

Required:
- Signature interactions documented with fallback equivalents.
- Experience map and module sequencing approved.
- Brand and motion direction approved.

Exit criteria:
- Every immersive module has a business purpose and conversion linkage.
- All critical content can render without 3D dependency.

### Gate 3: Technical Feasibility Sign-Off

Required:
- Performance budgets approved.
- Device tier and enhancement strategy approved.
- Accessibility and resilience strategy approved.

Exit criteria:
- Implementation plan satisfies performance guardrails.
- Fallback behavior validated for constrained environments.

### Gate 4: Production Readiness Sign-Off

Required:
- Content and asset matrix complete.
- Component and module backlog prioritized.
- QA and launch criteria approved.

Exit criteria:
- No critical dependency unresolved.
- Delivery timeline and owner accountability agreed.

## Prioritization Stack

### Must-Have

- Business narrative clarity.
- Performance-compliant hero and key proof modules.
- Conversion path with qualified lead capture.
- Accessibility-safe core journey.

### Should-Have

- Additional interactive case decomposition modules.
- Dynamic personalization for CTA framing.
- Enhanced motion transitions between sections.

### Wow-Layer

- Advanced real-time scene effects for high-capability devices.
- Experimental interaction flourishes with strict fallback parity.

## Dependency Matrix

- Narrative finalization -> capability and case module copy.
- Content proof availability -> case section credibility.
- Performance budget lock -> interaction complexity ceiling.
- CTA flow decisions -> form structure and lead routing.
- Fallback parity definitions -> accessibility and QA readiness.

## Delivery Timeline (Conceptual)

- Week 1-2: final strategy and narrative lock.
- Week 3-4: experience concept and visual interaction direction.
- Week 5: technical feasibility and budget validation.
- Week 6: production readiness review and kickoff decision.

## Risk Register and Mitigations

- **Risk:** visual ambition exceeds performance budgets.  
  **Mitigation:** enforce budget checks in concept approval.
- **Risk:** stakeholder misalignment on conversion objective.  
  **Mitigation:** lock KPI hierarchy at Gate 1.
- **Risk:** insufficient proof assets for enterprise confidence.  
  **Mitigation:** create early proof inventory and gap closure plan.
- **Risk:** fallback quality feels secondary.  
  **Mitigation:** design parity as explicit acceptance criterion.

## Go/No-Go Checklist

- [ ] KPI, persona, and narrative approvals complete.
- [ ] 3D interaction specs and fallback behavior approved.
- [ ] Performance budgets and monitoring strategy approved.
- [ ] Content and proof assets meet minimum launch threshold.
- [ ] Conversion flow and qualification model approved.
- [ ] Ownership model and timeline confirmed.

## Final Acceptance Criteria Before Build

- Every core module has: narrative intent, proof element, CTA logic, fallback behavior.
- No critical journey requires advanced graphics capability.
- Performance and accessibility standards are testable and assigned.
- Stakeholders agree on what constitutes a successful launch.

## Decision / Rationale / Implication

- **Decision:** proceed to production only through gate-based sign-off with measurable acceptance criteria.
- **Rationale:** this prevents premium creative direction from drifting into unscoped risk during build.
- **Implication:** planning artifacts become enforceable production constraints, not advisory documents.
