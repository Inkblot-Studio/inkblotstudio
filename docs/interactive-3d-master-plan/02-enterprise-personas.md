# Enterprise Persona Matrix

## Primary Persona 1: Economic Buyer

- **Typical titles:** CMO, VP Marketing, Head of Brand, Managing Director.
- **Core goal:** select a partner that improves brand perception and measurable business outcomes.
- **What they value:** strategic clarity, credibility, timeline reliability, business impact proof.
- **Primary fear:** expensive creative work that looks good but does not generate pipeline value.

### Objections

- "This feels too experimental for our stakeholders."
- "How do we justify this investment internally?"
- "Will this be maintainable after launch?"

### Decision Triggers

- Clear KPI framework tied to lead quality and brand lift.
- Evidence from case outcomes with measurable before/after impact.
- Strong governance model: scope, milestones, and risk controls.

### Messaging Implications

- Lead with business outcomes first, then visual innovation.
- Place trust anchors early (proof points, recognizable clients, measurable results).
- Keep CTA language outcome-oriented: "Plan your conversion-ready experience."

## Primary Persona 2: Technical Evaluator

- **Typical titles:** CTO, Head of Engineering, Technical Program Lead, Digital Platform Lead.
- **Core goal:** ensure technical feasibility, performance, scalability, and maintainability.
- **What they value:** architecture quality, performance budgets, accessibility, resilience.
- **Primary fear:** visually heavy implementation that causes performance regressions and maintenance overhead.

### Objections

- "3D scenes often break mobile performance."
- "This may create too much complexity for our team."
- "What is the fallback experience for constrained devices?"

### Decision Triggers

- Explicit performance guardrails and delivery budgets.
- Progressive enhancement strategy with graceful degradation.
- Modular implementation approach with documented ownership boundaries.

### Messaging Implications

- Surface technical discipline in process sections.
- Explain rendering strategy and fallback states in concise, confident language.
- Include engineering-facing proof: performance snapshots and accessibility readiness.

## Primary Persona 3: Creative Stakeholder

- **Typical titles:** Creative Director, Brand Director, Design Lead, Experience Lead.
- **Core goal:** launch a category-defining digital expression without losing brand coherence.
- **What they value:** distinctiveness, narrative, interaction craft, visual consistency.
- **Primary fear:** generic templates or over-optimized execution that loses emotional impact.

### Objections

- "Will this feel truly differentiated?"
- "Can we keep narrative depth without overwhelming users?"
- "Will the interaction language remain consistent across sections?"

### Decision Triggers

- Strong visual system and motion language standards.
- Story-first choreography where interactions reveal meaning.
- Clearly documented creative direction with sample interaction arcs.

### Messaging Implications

- Use editorial storytelling and cinematic pacing.
- Show experience principles, not only UI components.
- Frame 3D as narrative medium, not decorative effect.

## Cross-Persona Alignment Table

| Dimension | Economic Buyer | Technical Evaluator | Creative Stakeholder |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Primary success lens | ROI and pipeline quality | Performance and maintainability | Distinctive brand expression |
| Main risk concern | Investment without impact | Complexity and technical debt | Generic or diluted design |
| Required proof | Outcomes and case metrics | Budgets and architecture plan | Experience quality and coherence |
| CTA preference | Strategic consultation | Technical planning discussion | Creative direction workshop |

## Decision / Rationale / Implication

- **Decision:** prioritize a three-persona model with equal influence on planning decisions.
- **Rationale:** enterprise purchase decisions are cross-functional; single-persona messaging underperforms.
- **Implication:** each key page section must satisfy business, technical, and creative proof requirements in sequence.
