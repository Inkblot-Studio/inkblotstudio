# Discovery Interview Log - Session 1

## Session Objective

Capture foundational business decisions that define Inkblot's positioning, monetization strategy, and website conversion architecture.

## Inputs Collected

- Company: Inkblot Studio LTD.
- Core capability: local-first AI systems orchestration on high-performance hardware.
- Differentiator: privacy-sensitive delivery suitable for information-heavy clients.
- Product direction: Citron ecosystem under development, including Citron CRM with agentic workflows.
- Commercial need: prioritize near-term client revenue while building long-term product moat.

## Decision Register (Decision / Rationale / Implication)

### 1) Business Model Priority

- **Decision:** use a hybrid model with services-led revenue first and products as strategic moat.
- **Rationale:** immediate cashflow is needed while products mature.
- **Implication:** website must sell custom engagements first, with products framed as innovation proof and future platform path.

### 2) Primary ICP (First 12 Months)

- **Decision:** target mid-market, operations-focused companies first.
- **Rationale:** mid-market buyers have urgent process pain and shorter sales cycles than large regulated enterprise.
- **Implication:** messaging should emphasize operational speed, lower workflow friction, and measurable process outcomes.

### 3) Core Commercial Offer

- **Decision:** sell a Build + Retainer bundle.
- **Rationale:** bundle captures both implementation revenue and recurring optimization.
- **Implication:** conversion flow should qualify for both "initial build scope" and "ongoing optimization potential."

### 4) Geographic GTM Scope

- **Decision:** start local-region-first.
- **Rationale:** local credibility and references accelerate trust in early-stage brand building.
- **Implication:** website proof and CTA language should reference local partnership depth and rapid collaboration cycles.

### 5) Brand Personality

- **Decision:** lead with Luxury Innovator brand tone.
- **Rationale:** premium market positioning supports higher-value engagements and stronger differentiation.
- **Implication:** visual system must feel high-end and bold, but copy must remain commercially concrete.

### 6) Primary Conversion Goal

- **Decision:** optimize for `Book strategy call`.
- **Rationale:** consultative conversations are best for qualifying custom systems work.
- **Implication:** each major page module must ladder users toward one clear strategy-call CTA.

### 7) Proof Constraint

- **Decision:** operate with limited public case studies and rely on alternative trust architecture.
- **Rationale:** products and public outcome assets are early-stage.
- **Implication:** publish methodology, anonymized transformations, and technical governance artifacts as trust substitutes.
